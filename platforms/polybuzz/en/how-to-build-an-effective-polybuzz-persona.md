# How to Build an Effective PolyBuzz Persona

The Persona is the bot's memory of your player character.

It is not a character sheet intended for you as the player. Instead, it is a collection of persistent information that the bot uses to understand who your character is, how they function, and how they fit into the world.

However, the way you should build a Persona depends on one essential question: do you have access to PolyBuzz's Permanent Memory?

Without Permanent Memory, the Persona must contain virtually all of the important information about your character. Those 1500 characters become an extremely limited resource that must be used carefully.

With Permanent Memory, the strategy changes completely. Most of the character's stable information is already stored elsewhere, allowing the Persona to become a specialized complement rather than a complete character profile.

This guide explains both approaches.

---

# 💡 Which situation applies to you?

### 🚫 You do **not** have Permanent Memory?

Start with **Part 1**.

Start with **Part 2**.

# 🚫 Part 1 — Without Permanent Memory

## Why This Is the Most Restrictive Situation

Without Permanent Memory, the Persona becomes the bot's only persistent source of information about your player character.

Everything important must fit within **1500 characters**.

This limitation forces you to make choices. It is impossible to include a complete biography, a detailed personality, every relationship, and your character's full history.

The goal is therefore **not** to be exhaustive.

Instead, the objective is to preserve the information that allows the bot to immediately understand who your character is and interact with them consistently over long roleplay sessions.

This means learning how to prioritize information and accepting that some details will inevitably have to be omitted.

Think of the Persona as an optimized memory rather than a descriptive character sheet.

---

## What Should the Persona Contain?

Without Permanent Memory, the Persona must include all of the information that the bot genuinely needs.

Depending on the available space, you will generally try to include:

- your character's identity;
- appearance;
- personality;
- condensed backstory;
- important abilities;
- key relationships;
- behavioral functioning;
- residence, if it plays an important role.

In practice, it is rarely possible to develop every category equally.

Information must therefore be heavily condensed, keeping only what genuinely improves the roleplay experience.

For example, a few words describing your character's signature ability will often be far more valuable than a long explanation of how they learned it.

Likewise, a few carefully chosen details about important relationships usually provide more value than an exhaustive list of secondary characters.

The objective always remains the same:

**give the bot the greatest amount of useful information while using the fewest possible characters.**

## Organizing Your 1500 Characters

When you do not have Permanent Memory, the real challenge is not deciding **what** to write, but deciding **which information deserves to occupy the limited space available**.

Those 1500 characters disappear very quickly.

Instead of trying to give every category the same amount of space, prioritize the information that defines your character the most.

The information that permanently shapes your character should always come first.

For example, if you start running out of space, it is usually more valuable to keep a signature ability, an important relationship, or a defining part of your character's history than several secondary details.

Always ask yourself one simple question:

> **What does the bot absolutely need to know in order to immediately understand my character?**

Everything else becomes secondary.

Do not hesitate to heavily condense your writing.

The Persona is not meant to be pleasant to read.

It is an optimized memory written for an LLM.

Every character you save can be used to add another useful piece of information.

---

## Leaving Space for Important Events

Whenever possible, avoid filling all 1500 available characters from the very beginning.

Try to keep a small amount of free space so you can later record major events that permanently affect your character or their relationships.

For example:

- a lasting change in an important relationship;
- an event that profoundly affected your character;
- a development that now influences their behavior;
- an important decision whose consequences will continue to matter.

The goal is **not** to turn the Persona into a roleplay journal.

It should only contain events that the bot will still need to remember several scenes or conversations later.

This reserved space is, of course, very limited.

As your roleplay grows, you may eventually need to remove or further condense older information to make room for newer developments.

---

## The Limits of This Approach

Even when carefully optimized, a Persona can never fully replace Permanent Memory.

With only 1500 characters, it is impossible to preserve a rich history, numerous relationships, and every important long-term development.

This method simply allows you to preserve the information that matters most so the bot can continue understanding your character as consistently as possible.

The longer a roleplay continues, the more difficult it becomes to decide which information should remain and which must be removed.

If you regularly play long-term roleplays on PolyBuzz, Permanent Memory provides a significant improvement by taking over your character's stable identity and allowing the Persona to focus on complementary information instead.

This is the approach covered in the next section.

# 💾 Part 2 — With Permanent Memory

## What Changes

Permanent Memory completely changes the way you should build a Persona.

Without Permanent Memory, the Persona had to contain virtually everything about your player character.

Once Permanent Memory is available, this is no longer necessary.

A large portion of your character's stable information is now stored in Permanent Memory and automatically injected into every response generated by the bot.

The Persona is therefore no longer meant to function as a complete character profile.

Instead, it becomes a complementary memory whose purpose is to provide the bot with information that does not naturally belong in Permanent Memory.

This new distribution allows you to use the 1500 available characters much more efficiently.

Instead of repeating information that already exists elsewhere, you can dedicate the Persona to enriching the bot's understanding of your character during roleplay.

---

## What No Longer Needs to Be Written

Permanent Memory already stores several categories of stable information.

As a result, there is no reason to duplicate them inside the Persona.

Depending on how you use Permanent Memory, this generally includes:

- identity;
- age;
- appearance;
- condensed personality;
- residence;
- sexual orientation.

Repeating this information inside the Persona wastes valuable character space without providing any meaningful benefit.

Instead, every saved character can be invested in information that Permanent Memory cannot store as effectively.

---

## What the Persona Should Contain Instead

Once Permanent Memory handles your character's stable identity, the Persona can focus on information that directly improves the roleplay experience.

Typical examples include:

- important relationships;
- abilities;
- behavioral functioning;
- condensed backstory;
- useful contextual information the bot should always know.

The Persona becomes an extension of Permanent Memory rather than a replacement for it.

This approach generally produces much more consistent behavior during long-term roleplay.

---

## Functioning Is Not Personality

This distinction is important.

Personality describes **who your character is**.

Functioning describes **how your character thinks, analyzes situations, and makes decisions**.

For example, a few personality traits can easily be summarized inside Permanent Memory.

The Persona, however, can describe how your character actually operates.

For example:

> analyzes situations quickly; reads people well; identifies priorities rapidly; decides, then acts; adapts quickly; corrects mistakes rapidly; highly effective under pressure.

This type of information is extremely useful for the bot.

Rather than simply describing the character, it explains the character's cognitive and decision-making patterns.

As a result, the bot is far more likely to understand why your character reacts in certain ways and naturally adapt its own behavior during roleplay.

Functioning therefore complements personality.

It does not replace it.

## Writing Relationships Correctly

Relationships are one of the most valuable parts of the Persona.

They allow the bot to immediately understand where different characters stand in your player character's life without having to rebuild those relationships through conversation.

However, **not all relationships should be written the same way.**

---

### The Relationship with the Bot

The relationship with the bot should **always** be written **only from the player character's perspective**.

In other words, describe what **your character feels, thinks, believes, or has experienced** regarding the bot.

For example:

> Relationship with Kawaki: Drisnel is still genuinely attracted to him; their relationship was damaged by the way Kawaki handled what happened between them; despite that, she does not consider what they shared to be something trivial or easily forgotten.

This tells the bot how **your character** sees the relationship.

It does **not** tell the bot how **it** should feel.

Avoid writing statements such as:

- Kawaki is still in love with Drisnel.
- Kawaki completely trusts her.
- Kawaki deeply regrets what happened.

These are part of the bot's internal state.

They belong in the bot's own script and should evolve naturally through roleplay.

Defining them inside the Persona risks interfering with the bot's intended behavior or bypassing the progression built into its script.

---

### Relationships with Other NPCs

Other NPCs are different because they are **not** the bot.

Their relationships can therefore be written bilaterally if they are already established.

For example:

> Boruto: natural chemistry; honesty; loyalty; enjoys taking action together.

> Sarada: strong mutual respect; straightforward and stable relationship.

> Mitsuki: calm relationship; quiet mutual understanding.

These descriptions simply provide useful context about your player character's world.

They do not overwrite the internal state of the bot itself.

---

## Complete Example

The following example shows a Persona designed for use **with Permanent Memory**.

Because the character's identity, appearance, personality, and other stable information are already stored in Permanent Memory, the Persona can focus entirely on information that directly improves roleplay.

```text
Relationship with Kawaki: Drisnel is still genuinely attracted to him; their relationship was damaged by the way Kawaki handled what happened between them; despite that, she does not consider what they shared to be something trivial or easily forgotten.

Other relationships: Boruto, natural chemistry, honesty, loyalty, enjoys taking action together. Sarada, strong mutual respect, straightforward and stable relationship. Mitsuki, calm relationship, quiet mutual understanding. Naruto, sincere respect without idealization; appreciates his reliability and independence. Sasuke, clear and measured respect; mutual recognition of each other's abilities.

Abilities: prodigy of offensive Fūinjutsu; specialist in battlefield control; also proficient with Earth Release and Bōjutsu. Creator and sole user of Kagi, a staff containing more than one hundred preloaded seals, matrices, and complex systems deployed instantly through physical contact. Insei: medical seal located on the tongue; activated mouth-to-mouth; allows self-healing or direct healing of an ally.

History: prodigy of the Aoyama clan without being its heir; became a Jōnin at an early age; refused to join the ANBU; developed an unconventional offensive Fūinjutsu centered on battlefield control, improvisation, and tactical openings; continues serving Konoha while preserving her independence.

Functioning: analyzes situations quickly; reads people well; identifies priorities rapidly; decides, then acts; adapts quickly; dislikes stagnation; corrects mistakes rapidly; highly effective under pressure.
```

Notice that this Persona is **not** written as narrative prose.

Each category forms a compact block of information designed to be immediately usable by the LLM.

The objective is not to write a beautiful character description, but to communicate the maximum amount of useful information while using as few characters as possible.

# 🧠 Part 3 — Best Practices for Everyone

The recommendations in this section apply whether or not you have access to Permanent Memory.

They focus on **how** to write a Persona so it is easier for the LLM to understand and use effectively.

---

## Writing for an LLM

A Persona is **not** written for human readers.

It is written for an LLM.

Do not treat it as a biography, a wiki page, or a literary character description.

Instead, aim for writing that is:

- concise;
- information-dense;
- behavior-oriented;
- immediately usable by the model.

For example, prefer:

> analyzes situations quickly; reads people well; identifies priorities rapidly; decides, then acts.

Instead of:

> Drisnel is someone who analyzes situations very quickly. She understands people easily and usually makes decisions without hesitation.

The second version is more natural for a human reader.

The first version communicates more useful information while using significantly fewer characters.

---

## Using Punctuation Efficiently

Inside a Persona, punctuation is more than grammar.

It is also a way of structuring information.

The following convention has consistently produced good results during testing:

- **:** introduces a category.
- **,** links closely related information.
- **;** separates distinct ideas within the same category.
- **.** closes the category.

Try to avoid using multiple periods inside the same paragraph.

Each category should ideally remain **one continuous information block**.

Commas and semicolons allow you to organize information without breaking that block into several independent sentences.

---

## Optimizing Character Usage

The Persona is limited to **1500 characters**.

Every saved character can be invested in additional information.

When space becomes limited, consider:

- removing unnecessary spaces around punctuation;
- avoiding unnecessary linking words;
- shortening repetitive expressions;
- removing redundant information;
- prioritizing useful information over elegant writing.

The objective is not to make the Persona pleasant to read.

The objective is to communicate as much relevant information as possible.

---

## Common Mistakes

Some of the most common mistakes include:

- writing the Persona like a novel or biography;
- wasting characters on unnecessarily long sentences;
- confusing personality with functioning;
- defining the bot's thoughts or feelings inside the relationship section;
- duplicating information already stored in Permanent Memory;
- trying to include everything instead of prioritizing the most important information.

Remember that a Persona is an optimized memory.

Every line should serve a purpose.

---

## Observations from Practical Testing

The following observations come from extensive practical testing on PolyBuzz.

They are **not** part of the platform's official documentation.

Compared to Permanent Memory, the Persona sometimes appears to be used less consistently by the model.

Occasionally, the bot may seem to ignore information that is clearly present inside the Persona.

When this happens, simply editing the Persona—even by adding or removing a single character or space—and saving it again sometimes appears to refresh the Persona and restore its influence.

This behavior is not guaranteed, but it has been observed often enough to be worth mentioning.

---

# Conclusion

The Persona is one of PolyBuzz's most important features.

When built correctly, it allows the bot to understand your player character more accurately, react more consistently, and maintain stronger continuity throughout long-term roleplay.

The strategy you should use depends entirely on your situation.

Without Permanent Memory, the Persona becomes the bot's primary memory and must contain the essential information about your character despite the strict 1500-character limit.

With Permanent Memory, the Persona becomes a specialized complement that enriches the bot with information that Permanent Memory does not naturally store.

In both cases, keep one principle in mind:

**Write for the LLM, not for a human reader.**

The clearer, denser, and better structured your Persona is, the easier it becomes for the bot to understand your character and produce consistent roleplay over time.
